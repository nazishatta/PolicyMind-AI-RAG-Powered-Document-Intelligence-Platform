EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

This Strategy sets out a framework for reducing greenhouse gas emissions,
promoting renewable energy, and supporting an equitable transition to a
low-carbon economy. The objectives are aligned with internationally recognised
sustainable development principles and evidence-based climate science.

All signatory states commit to a minimum 55 percent reduction in greenhouse
gas emissions relative to 1990 levels by the year 2030, and to achieve net
zero emissions no later than 2050.

=============================================================================

PART I: POLICY OBJECTIVES AND SCOPE

Article 1 — Purpose and Scope

1.1 This Strategy applies to all member states that have ratified the
underlying framework convention. It establishes binding emissions targets,
renewable energy requirements, and compliance reporting obligations.

1.2 The Strategy is without prejudice to existing bilateral or multilateral
agreements that set more ambitious targets. Member states may adopt stricter
national measures consistent with this framework.

Article 2 — Definitions

For the purposes of this Strategy:

"Greenhouse gases" means carbon dioxide (CO2), methane (CH4), nitrous oxide
(N2O), and fluorinated gases as defined in the applicable annexes.

"Net zero" means a state in which greenhouse gas emissions from human activity
are balanced by removals from the atmosphere, measured on a 100-year global
warming potential basis.

"Just Transition" means the process of shifting to a low-carbon economy in
a manner that is fair and inclusive, creates decent work, and leaves no
community or worker behind.

=============================================================================

PART II: EMISSIONS TARGETS AND PATHWAYS

Article 3 — National Emissions Reduction Commitments

3.1 Each member state shall achieve, by 31 December 2030:
    (a) a minimum 55 percent reduction in total greenhouse gas emissions
        compared to the 1990 baseline;
    (b) a maximum emissions intensity of 0.15 kg CO2-equivalent per unit
        of GDP (purchasing power parity, 2015 prices).

3.2 Member states in the Industrial Transition Category (as designated by
the Secretariat) may apply for a phased compliance schedule, subject to
submission of a credible National Transition Plan by 30 June 2026.

Article 4 — Sectoral Targets

4.1 Energy sector: A minimum 30 percent share of renewable energy sources
in total final energy consumption shall be achieved by 2025, rising to
65 percent by 2030.

4.2 Transport: Zero-emission vehicles shall constitute no less than 50 percent
of new passenger vehicle registrations from 2028 onward.

4.3 Industry: Energy-intensive industrial facilities shall adopt best-available
techniques for emissions reduction and report annually under Article 9.

4.4 Land use and forestry: Member states shall maintain or increase their
forest carbon stocks relative to the 2020 baseline, and shall not convert
primary forest for agricultural purposes.

=============================================================================

PART III: JUST TRANSITION FUND

Article 5 — Establishment and Governance

5.1 A Just Transition Fund (the "Fund") is hereby established with an initial
capitalisation of EUR 15 billion for the period 2025–2030.

5.2 The Fund shall be governed by a Board comprising:
    (a) two representatives from each member state;
    (b) three independent experts nominated by the Secretariat;
    (c) one representative from an accredited civil society observer organisation.

5.3 The Fund prioritises support to:
    (a) coal-dependent regions and communities undergoing economic transition;
    (b) workers in fossil fuel extraction and related industries;
    (c) small island developing states and least developed countries.

Article 6 — Eligibility and Disbursement

6.1 Member states may apply for Fund support by submitting a Just Transition
Action Plan demonstrating:
    (a) identification of affected communities and economic sectors;
    (b) projected job losses and retraining needs;
    (c) planned investments in clean energy, infrastructure, and social
        protection.

6.2 Disbursement shall be performance-linked. States must report on
progress indicators annually. Persistent underperformance may result in
Fund support being suspended pending a compliance review.

=============================================================================

PART IV: MONITORING, REPORTING, AND COMPLIANCE

Article 7 — National Inventory Reporting

7.1 Each member state shall submit to the Secretariat a National Greenhouse
Gas Inventory no later than 31 March of each year, covering emissions and
removals for the preceding calendar year.

7.2 Inventories shall conform to the methodological guidelines established
in Annex III and shall be subject to independent technical review.

Article 8 — Transparency and Public Access

8.1 All National Inventories, Transition Plans, and compliance assessments
shall be made publicly available on the Secretariat's official portal within
30 days of submission.

8.2 Member states shall ensure that affected communities have meaningful
access to information about emissions, transition plans, and Fund disbursements
in accessible formats and relevant local languages.

Article 9 — Compliance Committee

9.1 A Compliance Committee shall be established to assess whether member
states are meeting their obligations under this Strategy.

9.2 Where a member state is found to be non-compliant, the Committee may:
    (a) issue a formal finding of non-compliance;
    (b) recommend corrective measures and a remediation timeline;
    (c) refer the matter to the governing body for further action under
        applicable provisions.

9.3 Member states found to be in material non-compliance for two or more
consecutive reporting periods may be subject to suspension of Fund access
and preferential trade arrangements, as determined by the governing body.

=============================================================================

PART V: FINAL PROVISIONS

Article 10 — Entry into Force

This Strategy shall enter into force 90 days after ratification by at least
two-thirds of the signatory states.

Article 11 — Review and Ambition Ratchet

The governing body shall convene a comprehensive review of this Strategy
every five years. Reviews shall assess scientific evidence on remaining
carbon budgets and consider whether targets should be strengthened in line
with the best available science and equity considerations.

Article 12 — Depositary

The Secretary-General of the regional intergovernmental body shall serve
as depositary of this Strategy and shall maintain a register of ratifications,
reservations, and notifications.
